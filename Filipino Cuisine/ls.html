Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai are crispy Filipino spring rolls filled with ground pork and vegetables. They are a popular party dish and are often served with a sweet and sour dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg ground pork
  • 1 cup carrots (grated)
  • 1 cup green onions (chopped)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 pack lumpia wrappers
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, combine ground pork, grated carrots, chopped green onions, and eggs. Season with salt and pepper. Mix well until combined.
  2. Wrap the Lumpia: Take a lumpia wrapper and place about 2 tablespoons of the filling near one edge. Roll tightly, folding in the sides as you go. Seal the edge with a bit of water.
  3. Fry: Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the lumpia in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Serve: Serve hot with sweet and sour sauce or your favorite dipping sauce. Enjoy your Lumpiang Shanghai!

Tips

  • For a healthier option, you can bake the lumpia instead of frying.
  • Add other vegetables like cabbage or bell peppers for added flavor and texture.
  • These spring rolls can be made in advance and frozen for later use!
