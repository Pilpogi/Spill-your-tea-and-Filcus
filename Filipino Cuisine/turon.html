Turon (Banana Lumpia)

Turon is a beloved Filipino snack made from ripe bananas (usually saba variety) and sometimes with a slice of jackfruit, wrapped in a spring roll wrapper and fried until golden and crispy. Itâ€™s often coated in caramelized sugar for extra sweetness!

Ingredients

  • 6 ripe saba bananas (or any sweet banana)
  • 1/2 cup ripe jackfruit, sliced (optional)
  • 6-8 spring roll wrappers
  • 1 cup brown sugar (for coating)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bananas: Peel the bananas and cut them in half lengthwise. If using jackfruit, slice it into thin pieces.
  2. Wrap the Bananas: Place a banana half and a slice of jackfruit (if using) on a spring roll wrapper. Fold the sides over the filling and roll tightly, sealing the edges with a little water.
  3. Coat with Sugar: In a shallow dish, spread the brown sugar. Roll each wrapped banana in the sugar to coat evenly.
  4. Heat the Oil: In a deep pan, heat enough oil for frying over medium heat.
  5. Fry the Turon: Once the oil is hot, carefully place the wrapped bananas in the pan. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes, turning occasionally.
  6. Drain and Cool: Remove the turon from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Caramelize (Optional): If desired, return the fried turon to the pan with a little sugar and cook until the sugar melts and coats the turon.
  8. Serve: Serve warm as a snack or dessert. Enjoy your delicious Turon!

Tips

  • Use ripe but firm bananas for the best flavor and texture.
  • Feel free to add a sprinkle of cinnamon to the sugar for added flavor.
  • Turon is best enjoyed fresh, but leftovers can be stored in an airtight container for a day or two.
