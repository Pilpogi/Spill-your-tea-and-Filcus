Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup made with pork and a variety of vegetables. The distinct sourness is traditionally derived from tamarind, making it a refreshing and comforting dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (belly or ribs)
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 cup long green beans
  • 2-3 green chili peppers
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (or 1 cup fresh tamarind juice)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the Pork: In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add the pork, onion, and tomatoes. Cook until the pork is tender (about 40-60 minutes).
  2. Add Vegetables: Add the radish, eggplant, and long green beans. Simmer for another 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add Sourness: Stir in the sinigang mix or fresh tamarind juice. Adjust the sourness to your taste.
  4. Add Chili: Add the green chili peppers and simmer for a few more minutes.
  5. Season: Taste and add salt as needed.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy your Sinigang na Baboy!

Tips

  • Feel free to add other vegetables like spinach or water spinach (kangkong).
  • For a different flavor, you can use other souring agents like calamansi or green mango.
  • Sinigang tastes better the next day as the flavors meld together!
