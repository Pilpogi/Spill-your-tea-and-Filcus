Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a rich and hearty Filipino stew made with oxtail, vegetables, and a thick peanut sauce. It’s typically served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) on the side.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup toasted ground peanuts (optional)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 banana heart, sliced
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 4 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Bagoong (shrimp paste) for serving

Instructions

  1. Boil the Oxtail: In a large pot, boil oxtail in water until tender, about 1.5 to 2 hours. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In another pot, heat oil over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add Broth and Peanut Sauce: Add the boiled oxtail along with the broth to the sautéed aromatics. Stir in the peanut butter and ground peanuts (if using). Simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add Vegetables: Add the banana heart, eggplant, and string beans. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 10-15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice and a side of bagoong. Enjoy your Kare-Kare!

Tips

  • For a thicker sauce, you can add more peanut butter or ground peanuts.
  • If you prefer a different protein, you can substitute oxtail with beef shank or tripe.
  • Make sure to adjust the seasoning to your taste!
