Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a savory and tangy Filipino dish made with chicken marinated in soy sauce and vinegar. It’s a beloved comfort food that is simple to make and incredibly flavorful.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (cane vinegar is ideal)
  • 1 head garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 2-3 potatoes (sliced)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces, soy sauce, minced garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for deeper flavor).
  2. Sauté: In a large pot or pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and brown the pieces on all sides.
  3. Add Marinade and Water: Once the chicken is browned, pour in the reserved marinade and add 1 cup of water. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender. If using potatoes, add them halfway through the cooking time.
  5. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. For a thicker sauce, increase the heat and let it reduce.
  6. Serve: Serve hot over steamed rice. Enjoy your Chicken Adobo!

Tips

  • Experiment with different meats, like pork or beef, for variety.
  • For a slightly sweeter taste, you can add a little sugar.
  • Adobo can be made in advance, and it often tastes better the next day!
